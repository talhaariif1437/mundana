Eiffel Tower is one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world, located in Paris, France. Standing at 324 meters (1,063 feet) tall, it is a global symbol of France and Parisian culture, attracting millions of visitors every year. Its unique design and impressive stature have made it one of the most iconic structures in modern architecture.

Marvellous Features of Eiffel Tower


1. History and Construction:
The Eiffel Tower was designed by the French engineer Gustave Eiffel and constructed between 1887 and 1889 as part of the 1889 Exposition Universelle (World‚Äôs Fair), held in Paris to celebrate the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution.

Eiffel's company was responsible for the design and construction, and it took just over two years to complete. The tower was initially criticized by many prominent artists and intellectuals in Paris for its unconventional design, but it quickly became an architectural and engineering marvel.

At the time of its completion, the Eiffel Tower was the tallest man-made structure in the world, surpassing the Washington Monument in the U.S.

2. Design and Structure:
The tower is made of wrought iron and weighs approximately 10,100 tons. It consists of over 18,000 individual iron parts that are held together by more than 2.5 million rivets.

The tower has three main levels, with the first and second levels offering restaurants and shops, while the third level (the observation deck) is at the top, providing breathtaking views of Paris.

The design is based on an iron lattice structure, which makes it not only lightweight but also incredibly strong and stable.

3. Views and Observation Decks:
The Eiffel Tower is one of the best places in Paris to view the city. The first and second levels offer fantastic views of landmarks like the Champs-√âlys√©es, the Louvre Museum, the Arc de Triomphe, and the Notre-Dame Cathedral. On a clear day, visitors can see all the way to Montmartre and beyond.

The third level, located at 276 meters, is the highest publicly accessible point in Paris. It provides an unparalleled 360-degree view of the entire city, with famous landmarks visible across the horizon.

The tower is also known for its nighttime illuminations, when it sparkles every hour on the hour, creating a magical sight against the Paris skyline.

4. Cultural Significance:
The Eiffel Tower has become an enduring symbol of French creativity, engineering, and modernity. It stands as a beacon of innovation and artistic achievement, and it has been featured in countless films, artworks, and photographs.

Initially planned as a temporary structure for the World‚Äôs Fair, the Eiffel Tower was almost dismantled after the event. However, it was saved due to its usefulness as a radio transmission tower.

Over the years, it has become one of the most photographed and visited monuments in the world, attracting millions of tourists from all over the globe. It is especially popular during romantic getaways, becoming synonymous with Parisian charm and elegance.

5. Visiting the Eiffel Tower:
Visitors can reach the top by stairs or elevator. The climb up the stairs is quite a workout, but it offers a unique perspective of the structure and is popular with those seeking a more physical challenge. The elevator, however, offers a quicker and more comfortable way to reach the upper levels.

There are restaurants on the first and second levels, including Le 58 Tour Eiffel (on the first level) and Le Jules Verne (on the second level), both offering gourmet dining with spectacular views.

The Champ de Mars, the large park surrounding the Eiffel Tower, is also a popular spot for picnics and relaxation, providing visitors with a chance to enjoy the tower from ground level.

5. Conclusion:
The Eiffel Tower stands as an emblem of both the artistic spirit and engineering innovation that Paris is known for. From its initial criticism to its present-day status as one of the most visited attractions on earth, the Eiffel Tower has become a timeless symbol of France and a must-see for any traveler to Paris. Whether you're taking in the panoramic views from the top, dining in its restaurants, or simply marveling at its elegance, the Eiffel Tower is a quintessential part of the Parisian¬†experience.
