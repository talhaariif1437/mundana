Wat Phra Kaew was established in 1782 by King Rama I, the founder of the Chakri Dynasty, as part of the construction of the Grand Palace. The temple was intended to house the Emerald Buddha, a highly revered statue of the Buddha, which is believed to bring prosperity and protection to the country.

The statue of the Emerald Buddha is seated in the Phra Ubosot, the central sanctuary of the temple. The figure of the Buddha is dressed in robes that are changed three times a year during the Thai traditional New Year (Songkran), the rainy season, and the cool season, each time symbolizing different aspects of the Thai royal calendar and agricultural practices.

Wat Phra Kaew is one of the most visited and important tourist attractions in Bangkok, drawing millions of visitors annually who come to admire the stunning architecture and the sacred Emerald Buddha.
